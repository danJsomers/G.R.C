Spyro

Spyro 3 is hands down the best 3D platformer of its time; with tight controls, good story and plot, great rewards for obtaining collectables and other playable characters. Spyro is a young, purple Dragon that is sent to retrieve the stolen Dragon eggs from the Sorcerer as he is the only one who can fit through the tunnels the thieves used to steal them. The player will venture through portals to unique worlds from underwater cities to magma filled volcanos. To get 100% of the game complete you have to beat some incredibly difficult challenges including timed challenges and trying to controll your way down a winding slide with no edges that is suspended above a bottomless pit! The reason it was so ahead of its time is how the landscape was coded to save memory. Basically, objects further away from Spyro had a lower polygon count and low texture graphics but as you got closer the polygon count and texture detail increased which created a very unique style.

Little Big Planet

LittleBigPlanet is a nicely stylised side scrolling yet 3D platformer. It is comprised of three layers in the Z axis for the first and second installments, as for the third, it has 16 layers! The protagonist is supposed to resemble a stitched cotton character that can be customised using collectables scattered around the story and the world it lives in are comprised of large everyday objects like it was on an adventure through your house using your objects to get around. Although there is a campaign, it is mainly focused on sharing community levels. Users can create their own levels for others using objects picked up in the campaign and their imagination.

Rayman

Rayman is a odd little character, he has a head, body, hands and feet but nothing connecting them together, no neck, arms or legs. His body parts that he does have sort of just float around where they would be if they were connected. This side scrolling 2D platformer is odd in its gameplay as well; Rayman can use his hair as a helicopter and extend his jump by doing so he can also throw his detached fist forwards to punch enemies from range. Later installments like Rayman Origins and Rayman Legends allow multiplayer with other wacky characters like a humanoid toad named Globox and little, blue, long nosed creatures named Teensies. In Rayman legends there are music levels where you do moves in time with the music playing in the background!
